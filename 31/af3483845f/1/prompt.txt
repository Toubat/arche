Important Decisions
No Effect dependency. Effect’s concurrency model is attractive, but too viral for this codebase.
No ctx parameter in coroutine bodies. Use io.context() opt-in access instead.
No ctx.spawn. Spawning remains explicit through io.coroutine(...).spawn() or io.spawn(...).
Coroutines are lazy and one-shot. Starting twice throws CoroutineAlreadyStartedError.
Cancellation wins. A cancelled coroutine cannot report success even if it catches CancelledError.
Spawn short-circuit. If spawned into an already-aborted scope, the body never runs.
Blocking-op short-circuit. An aborted scope cannot synchronously drain buffered values, take free permits, or resolve zero-counter waits.
Do not wrap data-structure ops in coroutines. That risks value loss because cancellation-wins could rewrite successful receives into CancelledError.
Channel commitment rule: a value is committed only once buffered or handed to a receiver. Parked sender payloads are in-flight and are dropped on sender cancel/channel close.
WaiterQueue is the shared blocking primitive. Structures own local state; WaiterQueue owns parking/waking/cancel-safe waiter removal.
